\bigskip
The difference between a case shortname 
\begin{quotation}
\noindent
(citation command \cmd{lccasesn} for fields shipname or partya),
\end{quotation}
 and a case short title 
 \begin{quotation}
 \noindent
 (citation command \cmd{lcshorttitle} for fields shipname, caseshortname, partya) 
 \end{quotation}
 is that a case short title is a user-defined shorthand for the case name.

For example, \lcinline{scc} [``\lcshorttitle{scc}''] can be referred to as \lcshorttitle{scc} after the first citation. Or in footnote format, once the case is introduced\footnote{\lcinline{mubili} [``\lcshorttitle{mubili}''].}, one can say: 

\begin{quotation}
\ldots as was said in \lcshorttitle[15]{mubili}: 

\begin{quotation}
``A leave decision merely permits a party to argue the issue. It is in no way a promise by the court to actually vary the order.''
\end{quotation}
\end{quotation}
